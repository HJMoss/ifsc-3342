About the Grant County Library

Our History

Established in April 1935 by a group of dedicated women concerned about the lack of books in Arkansas, the Grant County Library began in the Legion Hut. Mrs. Clarice Johnson Pollard served as the first librarian. Today, the library resides at 210 North Oak Street in Sheridan and is a proud member of the Mid-Ark Regional Library System.

Our Mission

Our mission is to provide quality materials and services that fulfill the educational, informational, cultural, and recreational needs of the community in a welcoming, respectful, and professional atmosphere, encouraging all to discover, dream, and thrive.

Library Board of Trustees

Established in 1978, the Board of Trustees serves as the governing body of the Grant County Library. Trustees are appointed by the County Judge and confirmed by the Quorum Court upon recommendation of the current Board and Library Director.

Friends of the Library

Founded in 2019, the Friends of the Grant County Library is a non-profit organization comprising volunteers, businesses, and organizations dedicated to supporting the library. Interested individuals can request a membership form at the circulation desk.

Mid-Ark Regional Library System

The Grant County Library is part of the Mid-Ark Regional Library System, which includes libraries in Cleveland, Dallas, Grant, Hot Spring, and Saline counties. Patrons can borrow materials from any of the five county libraries and the Bookmobile that makes regular stops throughout the region.
